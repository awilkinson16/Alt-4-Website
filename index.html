Initial Investigation

The task for your team is to design a product that will implement a microprocessor system that uses sensors and controls digital and analog, inputs and outputs as part of an embedded system. The product will be of value to those with an accessibility problem. This might include people who are elderly, blind, deaf etc. for whom mainstream technology can be challenging.

Definitions

Microprocessor

A microprocessor sytem consists of data input, storage, processing and output devices, under the control of a CPU.

Accesssibility Problems

People with cognitive difficulties, vision impairment or deaf people often have trouble accessing websites

Understanding The Problem

The task is to create something using microbits to aid people who have physcical or mental disabilitys.

Initial Ideas

1. Asking for discreet help in class

A child with learning disabilities may find it embarrasing to put their hand up to ask for help, we could use a microbit that they can have on them that when they press a button it will send a message to their teacher or SNA saying that they want help

2. Telling time reminder

A child that has a learning difficulty may have trouble telling time, we could create a microbit connected to a buzzer that has the students time table coded into it and will buzz to remind the student when to move lessons or when break time is over etc

3. Falling dectetor

For an elderly person living alone, we could put a microbit on them that will detect when they have fallen over and will alert the emergency services

4. Emotion aid

People who suffer with autism or other disabilities will sometimes find it hard to recognise emotions, we could have a microbit that can scroll through differnt faces e.g smiley, sad, angry and then once they've found the one that looks right they can click a button to display what the word for that emotion is

5. Elderly walking safety aid

For an elderly person living alone it is sometimes a daunting or scary task to even go on a walk alone. We could create a microbit that will track them, keep track of the amount of steps and warn them if they are doing too much excersise as elderly people often have weak bodys but sometimes do not realise it. It could also have a sensor that will detect if they have fallen and will send a message to the emergency services along with their location

6. Helping elderly keep in touch during the pandemic

Many elderly struggle to use smart devices and the internet, if we could have a microbit placed somewhere on the computer that with a simple button press would launch and call family it would be invaluable
